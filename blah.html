I flew 3500 Miles and all I got was InfoSec

Here's MY story about how I spent almost two weeks in Las Vegas for the 2022 Hacker Summer Camp. Enjoy!

In The Beginning there was...

It wasn't too long before I hit the tarmac, got off the plane and was shuffling into the Uber my Cybersecurity Career Accelerator (CCA) instructor, Chris Lemmon, ordered for us. The 25, or so, minute ride seemed to go quickly as we caught up from our last meetup and there I was, making my way between the Mandalay Bay and Luxor Casinos. And though it seemed like a blur, I needed to get to my hotel room and decompress. The real fun was just about to start.

Viva Las Vegas

After a quick shower and refresh, it's time to meet up with some folks and hit the town! Luckily the Black Hat Conference Associates (CA's) all communicate via the dedicated discord server. It was pretty cool sending out a chat that I'd be going to find some food and then walk the strip, than no sooner I got three and four pings responding they had just gotten to the City and would be happy to join. We took the inside hotel routes and found ourselves in a pretty amazing tropical installation.


And of course, it wouldn't be a Las Vegas trip without checking out the Bellagio Fountain. That was enough for the first night. Time to get ready for the Conference Associate orientation, bright an early. Afterall, I was there to work, right?


And you may ask yourself, "Well, how did I get here?"

Weil, I guess if Talking Heads want to know, then, let’s go back roughly a year. This is all thanks to a little organization called City Colleges of Chicago where I was finishing up my Associates Science in Computer Science. I’m a non-traditional student ready to make a pivot back into tech and found this amazing program that my local Community College called Cybersecurity Boot Camp; a 16 week intensive training course that prepares participants to take the industry-recognized CompTIA Security+ certification exam. I’ll tell you, this was a game changer for me and would STRONGLY encourage anyone who’s looking to make a change in their careers to consider YOUR local community college, as well. They are an incredible resource. Anyway, the four months went by, brain swollen with all the new information, and I took/passed the exam! I was hooked…


Another great thing about taking these courses are all the folks that come in as special guest speakers, including industry professionals, subject matter experts (SMEs) and alums; all happy to share their stories, guidance, encouragement and, best of all, RESOURCES! That’s when I first met Christy (yeah that person I linked WAYyy back in the beginning of this blog). She spoke about how she also shifted careers and was now facilitating the National Cyber League Capture the Flag program for Wilbur Wright College. I jumped at the opportunity and another 16 weeks later, I successfully completed my very first CTF, ranking 4th in the Cryptography section for Wright. And, more importantly, I increased my network by 20+ new cybersecurity enthusiasts/friends!
That then led me to another opportunity to participate in a pilot program called the Cybersecurity Career Accelerator (CCA); another 8 week intensive training program focused on providing an environment for personal networking, mentoring and elevating student’s career path entering into the cybersecurity industry. As a participant of this brand-new class, I had an opportunity to work as a Conference Associate (CA) for the annual BlackHat conference in Las Vegas. Oh, did I mention, they also provided a stipend for this trip? Yeah…So fast forward roughly a month and there I was, riding the Mandalay Bay escalator up to their Convention area to meet up with the rest of my 200+ CA’s that flew into the strip for this conference, too. So many green shirts!

Let there be CA's

Here’s my Chicago CCA crew, ready to take on this big convention together. Orientation was pretty straight forward; here’s what to expect, what to watch out for and what to do if you’re completely lost. We had lot’s of questions, for sure. As things went on, it became clear, the folks who were working behind the scenes of the Conference Associates program for Blackhat were a dedicated crew trying to make it all work. As a “new” CA my responsibilities were relatively chill. I was tasked with being the liaison for one of the many trainings being offered this year. My room was a two 2-day training on “Malware Analysis Fundamentals” sponsored by the folks at Mandiant. It seemed like a really cool subject but, unfortunately, we were not able to sit in on the trainings due to some rules and the optics that it gives off. Which made me wonder, how much did these trainings actually cost??? I’ll let you google it…HERE. Cough cough, $4,400 (not including admission to the Conference itself)! Either way, I made the most of it and being the personable kinda fella that I am, I struck up a couple conversations with the attendees to gauge how things were going and for the most part everyone seemed to really be enjoying the sessions; especially since their companies were paying for it, lol. And though I was advised to have my own personal ”business card” to share with people that I connect with, I found it just as easy to use the new QR code option that the LinkedIn App offers. Go ahead, give it a try (with App) and let's connect!

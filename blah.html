I flew 3500 Miles and all I got was InfoSec

Here's MY story about how I spent almost two weeks in Las Vegas for the 2022 Hacker Summer Camp. Enjoy!

In The Beginning there was...

It wasn't too long before I hit the tarmac, got off the plane and was shuffling into the Uber my Cybersecurity Career Accelerator (CCA) instructor, Chris Lemmon, ordered for us. The 25, or so, minute ride seemed to go quickly as we caught up from our last meetup and there I was, making my way between the Mandalay Bay and Luxor Casinos. And though it seemed like a blur, I needed to get to my hotel room and decompress. The real fun was just about to start.

Viva Las Vegas

After a quick shower and refresh, it's time to meet up with some folks and hit the town! Luckily the Black Hat Conference Associates (CA's) all communicate via the dedicated discord server. It was pretty cool sending out a chat that I'd be going to find some food and then walk the strip, than no sooner I got three and four pings responding they had just gotten to the City and would be happy to join. We took the inside hotel routes and found ourselves in a pretty amazing tropical installation.


And of course, it wouldn't be a Las Vegas trip without checking out the Bellagio Fountain. That was enough for the first night. Time to get ready for the Conference Associate orientation, bright an early. Afterall, I was there to work, right?


And you may ask yourself, "Well, how did I get here?"

Well, I guess if Talking Heads want to know, then let’s go back roughly a year; Fall of 2021. This is all thanks to a little organization called City Colleges of Chicago where I was finishing up my Associates Degree in Computer Science. I’m a non-traditional student looking to make a pivot back into tech and found this amazing program that my local Community College was offering Cybersecurity Boot Camp; a 16 week intensive training course that prepares participants to take the industry-recognized CompTIA Security+ certification exam. Let me tell you, this was a game changer for me and I would STRONGLY encourage anyone who is also ready to make a change in their careers, please consider YOUR local community college, as well. They are an incredible resource. Anyway, the four months went by, brain swollen with all the new information, and I took/passed the exam! I was hooked…


Another great thing about taking these courses are all the folks that come in as special guest speakers, including industry professionals, subject matter experts (SMEs) and alums; all happy to share their stories, guidance, encouragement and, best of all, RESOURCES! That’s when I first met Christy (yeah that person I linked WAYyy back in the beginning of this blog). She spoke about how she also shifted careers, currently as Advanced Cybersecurity Engineer pentesting for a pretty cool company and now also facilitating the National Cyber League Capture the Flag program for Wilbur Wright College. I jumped at the opportunity and another 16 weeks later, I successfully completed my very first CTF, ranking 4th in the Cryptography section for Wright. And, more importantly, I increased my network by 20+ new cybersecurity enthusiasts and friends!
That then led me to another opportunity to participate in a pilot program called the Cybersecurity Career Accelerator (CCA); another 8 week intensive training program focused on providing an environment for personal networking, mentoring and elevating student’s career path entering into the cybersecurity industry. As a participant of this brand-new class, I had the opportunity to work as a Conference Associate (CA) for the annual BlackHat conference in Las Vegas. Oh, did I mention, this was a paid gig (minumum wage), they also provided a travel stipend for the trip as well as paid hotel stay??? Yeah…So fast forward roughly a month into the program and there I was, riding the Mandalay Bay escalator up to their Convention area to meet up with the rest of my 200+ CA’s that were flying in from all around the Country for this conference, too. So many green shirts!

Let there be CA's

Here’s my Chicago CCA crew, ready to take on this big convention together. The orientation was pretty straight forward, "here’s what to expect, Here's what to watch out for and here's what to do if you’re completely lost." We had lot’s of questions, but mostly just in awe at all that was happening around us. As things went on, it became clear, the folks who were working behind the scenes of the Conference Associates program for Blackhat were a dedicated crew trying to make it all work. As a “new” CA my responsibilities were relatively chill. I was tasked with being the liaison for one of the many trainings being offered this year.


My room was a two 2-day training on “Malware Analysis Fundamentals” sponsored by the folks at Mandiant. It seemed like a really cool subject but, unfortunately, we were not able to sit in on the trainings due to some random rules I'm not totally clear on and the optics that it gives off. Which made me wonder, how much did these trainings actually cost??? I’ll let you google it…HERE. Cough cough, $4,400 (not including admission to the Conference itself)! Either way, I made the most of it and being the friendly and personable kinda fella that I am, I struck up a couple conversations with the attendees to gauge how things were going. For the most part, everyone seemed to really be enjoying the sessions. Especially since most were having their companies pay for it, lol. And though I was advised to have my own personal ”business card” to share with people that I'd be connecting with, I found it just as easy to use the new QR code option that the LinkedIn App offers. Go ahead, give it a try (thru the app) and let's connect!


We only come out at night

Even though there was a lot of standing during the early day’s of the Blackhat CA responsibilities, it started to pick up steam once the Business Hall opend up and the vendors had their booths open for "engagements". And after pulling 12 hour shifts, we were all looking forward to being released at the end of the day. It was Vegas, after all. I joined up with a couple of my new and old buddies to explore all the delicious food that was abound. There were so many happy-hours, networking events and after parties that I just jumped from one to another until enough people I knew were hanging out in one place, too. I know the old saying, “What happens in Vegas, Stays in Vegas” so I’ll just put a few photos up and the rest you’ll just have to join me next year!


DefCon30: Destination unknown

I don't think I was ready for what DefCon30 had to offer. It was like night and day from the last week I spent in the previous conference. Someone described it as Blackhat was where the parents went and DefCon was where all the troublemakers got together to have fun. And I couldn't agree more. This was the first time I was able to go outside during the daytime. BIG mistake. I finally got to feel the Vegas sun and boy was it oppressive. I think that's when I knew I couldn't live here indefinitely. In any case, off I went and by this time I had set up a good cadence with a couple others so that we’d all meet up to explore this new conference as attendees.

The people were awesome, the GOONS were super helpful and the Villages were so cool! I had my head on a swivel because everything was a brand new shiny ball. I couldn’t pick which one space I wanted to hang in and ended up jumping every hour or so. Luckily, my buddies kept me grounded and we got to do a couple CTFs. Here JAM made quick work of some password cracking challenges. He got a pretty cool t-shirt out of it, too!
I went for the more utilitarian route and upskilled my lockpicking technique. Not to mention I also got a chance to hang out with some NASA and SpaceX folks in the AeroSpace Village where I learned how to pwn a satellite. Used Ghidra for reverse engineering and sent a buffer overflow payload to make it spin uncontrollably. I can't tell you exactly all that I was doing but I can say it was AWESOME! Also, I think it was Needles who came in with the soldering skills to build up his killer DefCon Badge.


Don't cry because it's over, smile because it happened

We’ve come to the end of this blog but there is so much I left off. And not because I want to leave it off, no. On the contrary, I wish I had enough time to keep adding stuff. Like the amazing Dinner that Buford and I were picked to attend with THE NCL and Cyber Skyline folks! Or the fact that I not only got to meet Alyssa Miller but she also personally signed my copy of her book, “Cybersecurity Career Guide”. Oh, that’s right, and Nick Harbour also signed and gave me a copy of his book, too! But I must say, I was extra humbled by the kindness of Andrew van der Stock from the OWASP Foundation giving me a shirt and awesome Member Pin after I told him how I got my most recent job because I knew the latest OWASP Top 10 list. Yes, there were many curve balls, hiccups and downright logistical mistakes during BlackHat. But I gotta hand it to Casey B. I really think he tried his best to hear everyone out. I’m so glad I was able to attend both Blackhat's 25th Anniversary and DefCon’s 30th iteration. It’s the start of so many new adventures and I’m so looking forward to the ride. The swag wasn't bad, neither. Best Hacker Summer Camp Ever!

Happy Hacking!
